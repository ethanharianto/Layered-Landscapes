Stonehenge and Avebury

preservation of a mystery

background

Stonehenge and Avebury, like Blenheim Palace, have been declared a UNESCO World Heritage Site, fulfilling criteria (i) and (iii): “to represent a masterpiece of human creative genius,” and, “to bear a unique or at least exceptional testimony to a cultural tradition or to a civilization which is living or which has disappeared.” It also fulfills criteria (ii) as Blenheim does. We do not know, for sure, what these stone circles were used for, but the UNESCO site believes they were meant for ceremonial and mortuary use. Stonehenge is impressive in the size of the stones and precision used to construct its circle while Avebury’s stone circle is the largest in circumference; both are dated to between 3700 and 1600 BC which further adds to their mystery.
