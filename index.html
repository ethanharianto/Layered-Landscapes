Traces of the British Past

definitions

Cultural heritage includes artefacts, monuments, a group of buildings and sites, museums that have a diversity of values including symbolic, historic, artistic, aesthetic, ethnological or anthropological, scientific and social significance. It includes tangible heritage (movable, immobile and underwater), intangible cultural heritage (ICH) embedded into cultural, and natural heritage artefacts, sites or monuments. The definition excludes ICH related to other cultural domains such as festivals, celebration etc. It covers industrial heritage and cave paintings.[1]

Cultural identity is the definition of groups or individuals (by themselves or others) in terms of cultural or subcultural categories (including ethnicity, nationality, language, religion, and gender). In stereotyping, this is framed in terms of difference or otherness.[2]

the question

Throughout the quarter, we have visited various English heritage sites ranging from the ancient Stonehenge to the majestic Windsor Castle. We, as a class, were asked to ponder why these places were protected and preserved; some of these to varying degrees (think Avebury vs. Stonehenge). And so, for my final project, I wanted to observe the contribution that each of the places we visited makes towards English heritage throughout its and English history, prompting the question: do cultural heritage objects change meaning over time and if so, how may that contribute to the collective English identity?
